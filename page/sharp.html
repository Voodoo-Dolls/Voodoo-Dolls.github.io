Sharpshooter

The Sharpshooter is one of the precision perks available in Killing Floor 2. Sharpshooters excel greatly in killing larges and they can hold their own when it comes to trash. Players who are trying to learn the Sharpshooter perk should adopt a calm mindset because the perk is very difficult to learn.

+ Pros

  • One of the highest damaging Perks in the game
  • Only perk in the game that can kill 2 Fleshpounds without reloading
  • Their lvl. 25 skill can help trigger Zedtime with every headshot
  • Most of their skills mitigate recoil
  • Very high weapon switch speed
  • Cryogrenades offers huge Crowd Control (CC) ability

- Cons

  • Suffers from long reloads
  • Slow Movement
  • Constantly in a crouched position
  • Slow firerate during Zed Time
  • Has to be really accurate

M14

The M14 is a very well rounded weapon that can deal with trash and larges fairly well. Due to the its low-recoil, the M14 preforms well in almost any situation.

Since the M14 is very easy to use, many Sharpshooters adopt the M14 into their loadout.

FN FAL ACOG (FAL)

The FAL is the only fully automatic weapon available on the Sharpshooter. It's rapid fire capability makes the FAL the highest Damage Per Second (DPS) gun that Sharpshooter has to offer! However, due its extreme recoil, the FAL's effective range is only close up.

The FAL is a monster for any player who can tame the recoil and it's a great alternative to the M14.

SPX 464 Centerfire (SPX)

The SPX is a very powerful, but also a low recoil weapon. It offers insane DPS at the cost of reload speed. Due to its tube fed maganzine, the SPX should be reserved for only larges or any high priority Zed.

The SPX is actually Over-Powered (OP) in the right hands, but it is very difficult to master.

Winchester 1894 (Winchester)

The Winchester is the starting weapon for Sharpshooter. The performance is almost identical to the SPX, with the Windchester having a larger maganzine but lower damage per shot.

The Winchester is a great budget weapon since it can decap rioters in a single shot and deal with single larges when combined with a freeze. The base Winchester will stay relevant until Wave 4.

Mosin Nagant (Mosin)

The Mosin is one of the unique weapons in Killing Floor 2. It is the first gun to introduce a melee accessory and blocking functionality outside the Beserker perk. Due to its high damage per shot, the Mosin should be reserved for only larges or high priority targets; using only the melee for trash that gets close. One of biggest flaws with the Mosin is its slow reload and fire rate.

Since the Mosin can block. Players are suggested to place a Mosin on the kite to act as a tank for the team.

Freeze Grenade

The Freeze Grenade is what makes Sharpshooter unique in Killing Floor 2. It is the only grenade in the game that can freeze zeds reliably.

The Freeze Grenade should be used on crowds that may pose threat to the team while Kiting or to shutdown an inevitable push. Note: Fleshpounds and QPs are immune to freezes while raged and larges in general are immune to freezes while they are stumbled or knocked-down. More on Statuses HERE

Playstyle

In general, a Sharpshooter should attempt to aim for the head in order to make full use of their perk's passive Damage Bonus.

One commonly overlooked mechanic is the Level 25 Sharpshooter's ability to trigger ZED Time via their Ranger and Assassin skills. These skills offer a 5% chance to trigger ZED Time immediately upon scoring a headshot, which can be pivotal to the outcome of the game. Thus, it's in a Sharpshooter's best interest to score headshots as often as possible to increase the likelihood of this occurring. Sharpshooters in CD can be generally classified under two distinct playstyles:

  • Big Zed Hunter
  • Headclicker


Big Zed Hunter

Big Zed Hunters are the highest damage dealers of the Precision Perks outside of Zed Time. Sharpshooters who primarily use the SPX for their takedowns would be classified as a Big Zed Hunter.

Primarily using the SPX offers some major advantages in terms of DPS:

    +Pros

  • Decapitate (decap) any medium in a single shot
  • Decap Fleshpounds before they start charging
  • Decap Two Fleshpounds before reloading
  • Melt Scrakes like butter with Medic Buffs

But, primarily using the SPX comes with its own flaws:

    -Cons

  • Suffers in handling hordes of Zeds
  • Extremely long reloads. (Can be mitigated by 'V-Loading')
  • Very situational
  • Needs Intense Concentration to use effectively far ranges.
  • Less Shots = Less Zed Time

The Big Zed Hunter is best utlized when laned with a teammate who can take care of trash for you.


Headclicker

The Headclicker offers a more flexible Playstyle compared to the Big Zed Hunter. Since the Headclicker mainly uses the FAL or M14 as their primary weapon, they can effectively handle both trash and larges in any situation.

    +Pros

  • Can be used in any situation
  • The prefered Playstyle when soloing a lane
  • Easiest Playstyle to adopt
  • More Zed Time Activations

    -Cons

  • Practically None

Despite the Headclicker having no real downsides. There is some situations where the Big Zed Hunter is better than a Headclicker and a master Sharpshooter should be able to switch Playstyles when the situation calls for it.

Common Loadouts

M14 +1/FAL & SPX +2

The M14 +1/FAL & SPX +2 is the current meta for Killing Floor 2 CD. It offers high flexibility with the M14 or FAL and crazy burst DPS with the SPX.

Players are encouraged to buy SPX wave 2 to deal with early larges. Afterwards, players can decide whatever route to take to complete the loadout depending on the situation.

Mosin & M14 +1

The Mosin & M14 is another excellent loadout available for the Sharpshooter. The M14 offers the main DPS for the loadout, with the mosin acting as a sniper for long range take downs and off-perk tanking.

Players should grab the M14 first because the Mosin itself is more of a tool than a weapon.

Tips & Tricks

  • Freeze into double FP takedown

    One of the strongest attacks you can do with Sharpshooter is to freeze a group of fleshpounds, then quickly dispatch two fleshpounds with the SPX +2.

  • Rage Cancel into Freeze

    Don't suggest doing this often since people can potentially rerage the fleshpound and potentially kill you, but it is a handy trick to know.

  • Let Your Team handle the Front!

    One of the biggest advantages that Sharpshooters have would be their range. If you're laned with other teammates; consider shooting stuff that's in the back instead of the front. This allows you to thin out the horde and take out high priority targets such as Fleshpounds or Husks.

  • "Tactical Reload"

    If you're kiting as a Sharpshooter. You can still gain the reload bonus from Stability. In order to preform this trick, simply crouch , tap the reload key, then continue kiting.

    Note:Tube fed weapons only the gain the benefit for one round only.

  • You Become a Gunslinger While Standing!

    Being a Sharpshooter doesn't mean you are bounded to the floor. Unless you have 'Bone-Itis. Sharpshooters are basically mini gunslingers while standing. Their damage potential stays relatively high without the Stability skill active (very useful while kiting).

  • You Can Actually Freeze Bosses!

    While most zeds can be frozen with one Freeze Nade, bosses actually need 2-3. There's kind of two requirements for a boss to be frozen. The first one would be what phase the boss is in and the second one would be if they are in a 'vulnerable animation'. More on this in the boss section.

  • Protect Your Teammates!

    Since most Sharpshooters are in the back, they typically take less damage. If you have armor left, body block Fleshpounds for teammates who are about to die. Refer to body blocking HERE
