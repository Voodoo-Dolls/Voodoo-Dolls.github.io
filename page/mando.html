Commando

Also known as the ‘Enabler Perk’, the Commando is one of the precision perks available in Killing Floor 2. Commandos excel in killing trash and they can handle 1-2 larges if they have an adequate amount of space between them.


Commando is probably one of the easiest perks to get into, but one of the hardest to master.

Pros

  • + Can extend ‘Zed Time’
  • + Excellent Trash Clearer
  • + Fast Weapon Switch Speed
  • + Easy to Learn
  • + Can Handle Some Larges
  • + More HP than Average (Tenacious)
  • + See Cloaked Stalkers
  • + See HP Bars

Cons

  • - Slow movement speed
  • - Struggles Against Larges in Close Quarters
  • - Tough to Micromanage Reloads

Weapons

Commandos usually specialize in the Assualt Rifles weapon class. These weapons offer great trash control and decent large killing potential.

For Ammo Managment, both Tactical Reload and High Capacity Mags are viable. However, due to the fact that most Commando guns can be reload canceled, High Capacity Mags is considered the better choice in most scenarios.


Also, some weapons can decap larges from the extra ammo gained from High Capacity Mags without reloading.

For Tactics, Fallback is a clear winner over Impact Rounds. Fallback offers too much benefits that are practical in any situation. While Impact Rounds can mess up Scrake take downs and instantly killing Bloats isn't really useful for the most part.

For Survival, Commandos should always pick Tenacious because it can help Commandos absorb damage due to their slower movement speed. Also, Prepared is available for Overcapping, so the benefits of both can still occur.

For Weapon Specialist, Hollow Point Rounds is a clear winner. The skill itself Allows Commandos to engage in larges. Commandos can still receive the bonus with Eat Lead for one magazine by Overcapping.

For Advanced Training, ZED TIME - Machine Gunner is just better in every way. The extra damage and faster shooting makes Extensions a lot more easier in general. With the extra fire-rate, you can also juggle a Large take down and extensions at the same time.

Playstyle

In general, the Commando should be a dedicated trash killer and should be placed in any trash heavy lane. Commandos that face many larges should be laned with a dedicated large killer or have a teammate available as a flex at the very minimum.


When Zed-Time occurs, the Commando should focus on keeping the Zed-Time going as long as possible; this will enable players to dish out crazy amount of damage and potentially take back a jeopardized hold.


Commandos that choose the HMTech-401 Assault Rifle as part of their loadouts, gain a secondary role as a backup healer whenever the Medic dies or for no Medic runs.
